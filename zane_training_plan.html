Everything in this plan is built around unlocking those first, then stacking speed on top of a solid foundation.

Training Modules

01

ENGINE START

DYNAMIC WARMUP  ·  5–6 MIN

EVERY SESSION ▼
DRILLSETS / REPSXP
World's Greatest Stretch
Step into a lunge, drop the back knee, reach same-side arm to sky. Opens hips and thoracic spine before any load. Non-negotiable given his hip tightness.
5 each side+10 XP
Leg Swings — Front/Back then Side/Side
Dynamically warms the hip joint and begins waking up the hip flexors before sprint work starts.
10 each direction, each leg+10 XP
Ankle Alphabet
Sit and trace the alphabet with each foot. Sounds silly, earns big XP. Critical warmup for his tight ankles before loading them.
Full alphabet, each foot+15 XP
Bear Crawl
Full-body warmup that also activates core and shoulder stability needed for catching and football contact.
2 x 10 yards+15 XP
COACH TIPTime the ankle alphabet and let him try to beat his record each week. Kids love chasing their own numbers.
02

UNLOCK THE JOINTS

HIP + ANKLE + FOOT FLEXIBILITY  ·  10 MIN

EVERY SESSION ▼
STRETCH / DRILLHOLD / REPSXP
Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch
The most important stretch in this plan. Directly targets tight, weak hip flexors. One knee on the ground, drive the hips slightly forward and hold. This is the engine upgrade.
30 sec each side, 2 rounds+20 XP
Deep Frog Squat Hold
Mimics his catcher's stance almost exactly. Opens the groin, inner hip, and ankles simultaneously. Tell him this is how catchers build their superpower stance.
3 x 20–30 sec hold+20 XP
Tall Kneeling Hip Shift
Kneeling upright, shift hips slowly side to side. Teaches the hips to move independently, directly improving cutting and lateral movement in both sports.
10 slow reps each direction+15 XP
Seated Bent-Knee Calf Stretch
Ankles won't loosen if the calf is locked. Bent-knee version hits the deeper soleus that limits ankle mobility and contributes to flat-foot stomping.
30 sec each side, 2 rounds+15 XP
Wall Ankle Mobilization
Stand one foot from a wall, drive the knee toward it while keeping the heel flat. Unlocks ankle dorsiflexion required for acceleration, jumping, and a proper midfoot strike.
10 reps each ankle, 2 rounds+20 XP
Towel Toe Curls + Short Foot NEW
Sit barefoot, put a small towel on the floor and scrunch it with his toes. Then "dome" the arch without curling the toes (Short Foot). Builds the intrinsic foot muscles that create a springy, active landing platform instead of a dead flat stomp.
Towel: 3 x 10 curls. Short Foot: 3 x 10 holds (3 sec each)+20 XP
FROG SQUAT RECORDEach week try to add 5 more seconds to the hold. Keep the personal record on a whiteboard. Tie the improvement to a promise: a better frog squat hold equals a faster sprint, because it is true.
03

SPEED PROTOCOL

SPRINT MECHANICS + ACCELERATION  ·  10 MIN

MON + FRI ▼
DRILLSETS / REPSXP
Slow-Motion High Knees (Exaggerated)
Go slow on purpose. Drive the knee as high as possible and lower it even slower. Recruits hip flexors that are currently underperforming. Speed comes after the pattern is locked in.
3 x 15 yards+20 XP
A-Skip with Toe Pull (Paw Drill) NEW
On each skip, actively pull toes toward the shin before the foot hits the ground, then contact with the ball of the foot only. Exaggerate it. This drill directly rewires flat-foot stomping by forcing dorsiflexion and a quick midfoot strike as a habit before sprints start.
3 x 15 yards+25 XP
Skipping for Maximum Height
Builds the knee-drive and arm-swing pattern needed for sprinting. Tell him to try to touch the sky with each skip. Bigger is better.
3 x 20 yards+20 XP
Wall Drive Holds
Hands on wall, 45-degree body lean, one knee up. Hold 3 seconds, switch. Builds the starting position for an explosive first step that shows up in outfield jumps and defensive play.
3 x 5 each leg+15 XP
10-Yard Burst Sprints — "QUIET FEET" CUED
Short enough that every rep is pure acceleration. Before each rep, the coaching cue is "quiet feet." Flat-foot stomping is loud — challenge him to sprint 10 yards and make it sound like nothing. Frame it as a ninja challenge. If you can hear him, he's losing speed to the ground.
6 x 10 yards, full effort+30 XP
Reaction Sprint — Coach Calls Go
Stand still, eyes forward. Coach calls "GO" or taps shoulder randomly. First step must be explosive. Football-ready reflex training.
6–8 reps, 10 yards+25 XP
SPEED CHALLENGETime his 10-yard dash every two weeks. Write it down. Film Day 1 for the before/after comparison later. Even dropping from 2.4 to 2.2 seconds is a massive gain for a 9-year-old — make sure he knows that.
04

AGILITY ARENA

LATERAL QUICKNESS + DIRECTION CHANGE  ·  10 MIN

WED + FRI ▼
DRILLSETS / REPSXP
Lateral Shuffle + Hop
5 shuffles left, plant and hop, 5 right, plant and hop. Teaches hip loading for direction changes that show up fielding ground balls and chasing football routes.
4 x 20 yards+20 XP
5-Cone Star Drill
4 cones in a square, 1 in the middle. Coach points to a cone — sprint there and back. Hits reaction, acceleration, and deceleration in the exact patterns needed in both sports.
8 timed reps, 10 sec rest+30 XP
Defensive Drop Step
Ready position. Coach points left or right. Drop that foot back and open the hips to run. Essential for reading fly balls in the outfield and playing coverage in football.
3 x 8 each direction+25 XP
Tennis Ball Drop Reaction
Drop a tennis ball from shoulder height. Zane catches it before the second bounce. Progress by lowering the drop height over time. Pure reflex training — catchers and receivers live by this.
10 reps each hand+20 XP
Pogo Hops NEW
Both feet together, small quick hops in place. Goal is to spend as little time on the ground as possible. Minimal knee bend, all ankle spring. Teaches the ankle to act like a loaded spring instead of a dead weight — the exact correction for flat-foot stomping. Land soft and bounce fast.
3 x 20 hops+20 XP
Box Jump (8–12" box)
Two-foot takeoff, two-foot landing, stick it for 2 seconds. Builds explosive hip extension and ankle stiffness. Land soft as a cat, jump as hard as possible.
3 x 5 reps+25 XP
BOSS FIGHTThe 5-Cone Star is his weekly boss fight. Time it every session and post the record. Sub-5 seconds is elite status for a 9-year-old. Name it the Zane Star Challenge and let him chase it.
05

HIP POWER STATION

HIP FLEXOR + GLUTE ACTIVATION  ·  8 MIN

MON + WED ▼
DRILLSETS / REPSXP
Standing Knee Drives (Slow and Controlled)
Hold a wall for balance. Drive one knee to waist height as slowly as possible, lower even slower. Isolated hip flexor work at a kid-safe load. Call it slow-motion running in place.
3 x 12 each leg+20 XP
Glute Bridge with Knee Drive
Bridge up, hold, then drive one knee toward the chest for 2 seconds. Works the glute on the base leg and the hip flexor on the driving leg simultaneously. Two upgrades in one move.
3 x 10 each side+25 XP
Banded March in Place
Light resistance band at the ankles adds load to the hip flexors without joint risk. Start bodyweight for weeks 1–2, then introduce a band in week 3.
3 x 20 steps each leg+25 XP
Slow Mountain Climbers
Plank position, alternate driving knees on a 3-count each rep. Slower is harder and more effective for hip flexor activation than going fast. Have him count out loud.
3 x 10 each side+20 XP
LEVEL UP MOMENTAfter 4 weeks, film the 10-yard sprint again and compare to Day 1. The difference in knee lift will be visible. Show him the side-by-side. That is the single most motivating thing you can do for a young athlete.

Passively stretches the hip flexor with a balance challenge built in. Good for ankle proprioception as a bonus.
30 sec each side+10 XP

20 seconds each variation each side. Directly supports ankle flexibility gains from Module 2 and the foot strike improvements from Module 3.
20 sec x 2 variations, each side+10 XP

07

LADDER + HURDLE LAB

FOOTWORK PRECISION + ANKLE SPRING  ·  10 MIN

WED + FRI ▼
DRILLSETS / REPSXP
Ladder — Two Feet In Each Rung (Slow First)
Both feet land inside every rung stepping left-right-left-right. Start slow enough that every footfall is deliberate and quiet. This is the foundational pattern — don't rush it. If he's stomping through the rungs, slow down until he isn't. Speed comes in week 3+.
4 x full ladder length, slow build+20 XP
Ladder — Lateral In/Out (Side Shuffle)
Stand beside the ladder, step both feet in then both feet out, moving laterally down the length. Directly trains the lateral hip loading pattern for fielding and football coverage, while forcing quick light foot contacts on every step.
3 x full ladder, each direction+25 XP
Ladder — Ickey Shuffle
Two feet in, one foot out to the side, advance one rung, repeat. The king of coordination drills for young athletes. Goes directly into the session log as a timed "clean run" once the pattern is clean. Don't time it until the footwork is consistent — form before speed, always.
3 x full ladder, timed when clean+30 XP
Hurdle Hops — Two Foot Sequential
Jump over each hurdle with both feet, sticking the landing for 1 second before jumping the next. Replaces the box jump and is safer for his age — no hard edge to misjudge. Builds explosive hip extension and the ankle stiffness that directly counters flat-foot stomping.
3 x 5 hurdles in sequence+25 XP
Hurdle Hops — Single Leg (Low Hurdle Only)
Hop over each hurdle on one foot, landing and immediately hopping the next. One leg at a time. This is the ankle spring builder that connects directly to his pogo hop work and is the single best drill for eliminating the flat-foot pattern under load. Start with the lowest hurdle height.
2 x 4 hurdles each leg+30 XP
Ladder Sprint Out
Run two feet in each rung through the full ladder, then explode into a full 10-yard sprint the moment he exits the last rung. Connects the footwork precision of the ladder directly to acceleration. The contrast between controlled ladder steps and the sprint exit is the point — it teaches his nervous system to shift gears.
4 x full ladder + 10-yard sprint+25 XP
LADDER RULE #1Quiet feet self-corrects here. If he stomps through the rungs he trips or loses rhythm. You don't need to cue it as much — the ladder punishes the stomp naturally. Your job is to slow him down until the footwork is clean, then let the speed come on its own.
PROGRESSIONWeeks 1–2: two feet in each rung only, slow and clean. Week 3: add lateral in/out. Week 4: introduce ickey shuffle. Week 5+: all patterns plus timed runs and sprint-outs. Hurdle single-leg hops stay at the lowest height until he can do 4 consecutive without a stumble.
FOOTBALL NOTE Tackle football adds collision forces baseball doesn't. The lateral strength work and drop-step drills directly prepare him to absorb and redirect contact. His glute and hip strength are also the primary tools a ball carrier uses to break tackles. The hip flexor work in this plan is literally making him harder to bring down.
CATCHER + OUTFIELD NOTE The frog squat and hip mobility work will noticeably improve how comfortable his catcher's stance feels within 3 to 4 weeks. For outfield, the drop step and reaction sprints are the exact first two movements a center fielder makes when reading a ball off the bat.
FLAT-FOOT NOTE Tight ankles and weak hip flexors both contribute to stomping because when the ankle can't dorsiflex and the knee can't drive up, the foot falls heavy and flat. The Towel Toe Curls, A-Skip with Toe Pull, Pogo Hops, and the "quiet feet" sprint cue all target this directly. As ankle mobility improves through Module 2, the flat-foot pattern will naturally begin to correct itself on top of those additions.

8-Week Progression Map

WEEKS 1–2  ·  BUILD THE BASE

Every session opens with the full flexibility module — no shortcuts. Intensity stays low to medium. The job is to get Zane comfortable with the movement patterns before speed pressure is added. Record a 10-yard sprint and a frog squat hold on Day 1 for comparison later.

INTENSITY40%
WEEKS 3–4  ·  LOAD IT UP

His hips and ankles will already feel more open. Introduce a light resistance band on the hip flexor work. Add 2 sprint reps per session. Make agility drills timed and compete against previous scores. Reaction drills get faster and less predictable. The pogo hops should feel noticeably bouncier.

INTENSITY65%
WEEKS 5–8  ·  GAME SPEED

Full sessions at near-game intensity. Add sport-specific elements — reading a fly ball before the drop step sprint, firing out of a linebacker stance. Introduce 20-yard sprints alongside the 10s. Do the Before vs. After video comparison and celebrate it visibly. The flat-foot stomp should be largely gone by week 6 if the mobility and pogo work has been consistent.

INTENSITY85%
WHAT TO LOOK FOR

Week 2: Ankle alphabet gets faster. Frog squat hold gets longer. Week 4: Visible knee lift improvement in high knees. Sprint sound gets quieter. Week 6: Drop step opens cleanly with no hip restriction. Pogo hops sound light. Week 8: Side-by-side video comparison of Day 1 sprint vs current. Visible and dramatic difference in form, knee drive, and foot strike.
